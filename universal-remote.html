How could a mobile app replace traditional remotes?

With a stereo, cable box, TV, and dvd player, you'd have to switch between four remotes. That’s crazy.

Now people can do everything from adjust their home's temperature, to summoning a black car for transport. People are becoming more comfortable using a single mobile devices to control the world around them. TV remotes are archaic, overcomplicated, and a TV control app would put less between them and their content.

A Survey of the prospective audience

Through a Typeform survey and mechanical turk, I found what was frustrating people
View the results

The good

  • I like that the remote has glow-in-the-dark buttons, which makes it easy to see in the dark. I also like the shape of it. It feels very good in my hand.
  • The remote controls all of the devices off one remote. Pressing the power button turns off the TV and the cable box together.
  • On/Off buttons, changing displays, controlling volumes…

The bad

  • The fact that I need two remotes to properly control my devices
  • There are too many buttons that I never use, which get in the way sometimes.
    It took a while for me to remember where some of my go-to buttons were.
  • There are a lot of buttons that I never use. I don't know what they do. I would like bigger buttons.
  • My universal remote doesn't work with the blu ray player
  • They get lost and drink batteries
  • 100% of people had a TV in their home
  • 96% of people watched TV within the last week
  • 30% of people used at least two remotes

From the survey, I knew I had to address the initial set up and inutitive, relevant, interface.

Mental model

He has separate remotes for his stereo, cablebox, and TV. He is frustrated because he doesn't like finding, and switching between all the remotes. He is tired of replacing all of the batteries. He has downloaded a universal remote control app, to help him get past all of the annoyance of the traditional remotes

Competitors

Reviews from the app store helped inform this

Peel

Experience personalized TV, with recommendations just for you!

Pros

  1. TV guide of upcoming shows
  2. Personalized TV recommendations
  3. Controls both TV and cablebox

Cons

  1. No volume control for a separate stereo box
  2. No DVD control, only TV and cablebox.
  3. No mute button

Roomie

Control your home theater components supporting thousands of devices

Pros

  1. Optomizes UI based on synced remote
  2. Cable, satellite, and antenna television listings
  3. Macros for complex command timing for automation

Cons

  1. Frustrating first time UX, lack of documentation
  2. Requires monthly subscription for infrared devices
  3. Each additional control of a remote is $25

Logitech

The world’s largest remote control compatibility databases with over 225,000 home theater devices.

Pros

  1. Lots of device compaitbility, including Roku, projectors, or a PS4
  2. Easy set up
  3. Gestures actions for power users

Cons

  1. Requires it to be paired with a Harmony Ultimate, or Harmony Smart Control
  2. Customizing activities is limited on the app as opposed to the MyHarmony site
  3. Segmented controls between use cases
  4. Requires internet connection to work

Persona

Jim lives in the suburbs of long island and recently got a cable-subscription for his new TV. He is frustrated by the number of removes he has to use. One to power the system on and volume, and the other to change the channels on the cable box. He has to use a third remote to use his DVD player.

Use cases

  1. Bought a TV and cable subscriptions and setting up for the first time
  2. Integrating existing multiple remotes into a single app
  3. I want to turn on my tv, switch to dvd, and play a movie, and control the audio from one app
